Vinyl siding remains a top contender in Pullman Washington due to its affordability, low maintenance, and impressive weather resistance. Modern vinyl siding comes in a variety of styles and colors, making it easy to match your home’s aesthetic while protecting it from UV rays and moisture damage.
Wood Siding
For those who prefer a more natural look, wood siding can be a great option. However, in Pullman Washington it’s crucial to opt for treated or cedar wood, which naturally resists moisture and insects. Regular maintenance and staining are required to prevent rotting.

Metal siding, particularly aluminum and steel, is increasingly popular in Pullman Washington for its durability and resistance to extreme weather conditions. It’s also highly energy-efficient, reflecting sunlight and keeping your home cooler during the hot summer months.

Pullman, WA Insights: Population, Influence, and Service Areas

Pullman (/ˈpʊlmən/) is the largest city in Whitman County, located in southeastern Washington within the Palouse region of the Pacific Northwest. The population was 29,799 at the 2010 census, and estimated to be 34,506 in 2019. Originally founded as Three Forks, the city was renamed after industrialist George Pullman in 1884.
